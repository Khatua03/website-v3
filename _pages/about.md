## About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Scholar in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at the University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA). My research focuses on uncovering ultrafast phenomena in quantum materials and fusion plasmas using advanced optical and microwave diagnostics. I am particularly interested in understanding how light, charge carriers, and electromagnetic waves interact in complex systems across vastly different spatial and temporal scales.

My work spans ultrafast pump–probe spectroscopy of two-dimensional materials, high-intensity plasmonics, Doppler backscattering diagnostics, and ion cyclotron emission in magnetically confined plasmas. By combining experimental innovation, scientific instrumentation, and data-driven analysis, I seek to reveal the fundamental mechanisms governing nonequilibrium dynamics in materials and plasmas.

Beyond research, I enjoy developing scientific instrumentation, creating data-analysis tools, and mentoring students. My long-term goal is to establish an interdisciplinary research program that bridges materials science, photonics, and plasma physics to address fundamental challenges in energy, sensing, and next-generation quantum technologies.
