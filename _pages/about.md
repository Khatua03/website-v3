## About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Scholar in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at UCLA. My research explores ultrafast phenomena in quantum materials and fusion plasmas through advanced optical and microwave diagnostics. I am particularly interested in understanding how light, charge carriers, and electromagnetic waves interact in complex systems across vastly different scales.

My work spans ultrafast pump–probe spectroscopy of two-dimensional materials, high-intensity plasmonics, Doppler backscattering diagnostics, and ion cyclotron emission in magnetically confined plasmas. By combining experimental innovation with data-driven analysis, I aim to uncover the fundamental physics governing nonequilibrium processes in materials and plasmas.

Beyond research, I enjoy developing scientific instrumentation, creating data-analysis tools, and mentoring students. My long-term goal is to build an interdisciplinary research program that bridges materials science, photonics, and plasma physics to address challenges in future energy and quantum technologies.
